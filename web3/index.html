Intro

Bombay is my hometown, I grew up here.

Its a modern city with a tall skyline, one of the biggest cities in the world and the financial capital of India and the home of Bollywood, the largest movie industry in the world. Bombay got its name from Bom Baia which is Portugese for "Good Harbour".

by Tiger Puri, October 18, 2024

Gateway of India

Grand, Indo-Saracenic-style, 26m-tall triumphal stone arch, the Gateway was built on the waterfront in 1924. Popular with tourists to take water taxis to other places around the harbor.

by Tiger Puri, October 18, 2024

Elephanta Caves

Within Bombay harbor is Elephanta Island with an ornately sculpted, 7th-century cave temple dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva. The cave complexes around Bombay date back to 500 CE or 2500 years ago, making it one of oldest continuously inhabited places in the world.

by Tiger Puri, October 18, 2024

Victoria Terminus

This high Gothic Victorian landmark is the centerpiece of the city's suburban train network as well as a prominent railway station for cross-country train service.

by Tiger Puri, October 18, 2024

Marine Drive and Chowpatty Beach

Marine Drive is absolutely wonderful. The iconic promenade, also known as the "Queen's Necklace" due to its shape and the beautiful lights that line the curve of the bay, is truly a sight to behold. It is also famous for its Chowpatty Beach and is lined with one of the largest rows of Art Deco buildings in the world.

by Tiger Puri, October 18, 2024

Interesting Facts

Some interesting information about Bombay:

  1. Population: 21.6 million in 2024, it is the largest English speaking city in the world.
  2. Geography: Located on the West Coast of India, its the capital of the state of Maharashtra.
  3. Weather: The climate of Mumbai is warm and humid with monthly temperatures varying from 91 째F (33 째C) in May to 67 째F (19 째C) in January. Cool weather prevails from December to February and hot weather from March to May. The rainy season lasts from June to September and the post-monsoon season from October through November.
  4. Language: You need to know one of four languages in Bombay to survive: Marathi, Hindi, Gujarati, or English. Most of the jobs in Mumbai require a working knowledge of English. Bombay is also the city with the highest number of multilingual people in the world.
